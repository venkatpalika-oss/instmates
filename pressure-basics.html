Pressure Measurement – Basics

Pressure is one of the most fundamental measurements in any plant. Understanding the **type of pressure being measured** is critical before selecting or troubleshooting a pressure transmitter.

Types of Pressure Used in Plants

In field instrumentation, pressure is generally classified into three types: Gauge, Absolute, and Differential.

Field Explanation (Technician View)

  • Gauge pressure is used when atmospheric pressure is not critical (most plant services).
  • Absolute pressure is required for vacuum services and gas calculations.
  • Differential pressure is widely used for flow and level measurement.
  • Wrong pressure type selection leads to wrong readings even if the transmitter is healthy.

Common Field Mistakes

  • Using gauge transmitters for vacuum service
  • Ignoring atmospheric reference vent blockage
  • Confusing DP pressure with gauge pressure
  • Wrong impulse line connections on DP transmitters

Golden Rule

Always identify the **pressure reference** before selecting or troubleshooting a pressure transmitter. Most pressure problems are selection or installation issues, not transmitter failures.
