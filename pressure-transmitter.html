Pressure Transmitters (PT/DP) – Internals & Field Logic

A pressure transmitter converts process pressure into a standard signal (typically 4–20 mA). Understanding the internal construction helps technicians troubleshoot zero shift, slow response, unstable readings, and DP errors without unnecessary replacements.

Typical Smart Pressure Transmitter – Cutaway View

Most smart transmitters use an isolation diaphragm and fill fluid to protect the sensor. DP transmitters have two pressure ports (High/Low) connected to the sensing element.

What Technicians Must Remember

  • Isolation diaphragm protects the sensor from corrosive / dirty process.
  • Fill fluid transfers pressure — temperature changes can cause slight zero shift.
  • DP transmitters measure H − L. Wrong tubing causes negative readings.
  • Most “transmitter faults” are actually impulse line, manifold, or process problems.

Common Field Issues & Quick Checks

  • Zero shift after shutdown → temperature swing, trapped pressure, wet impulse line.
  • Sluggish response → plugged impulse line, partially closed manifold, sticky root valve.
  • No change in reading → blocked port, closed isolation valve, frozen impulse line.
  • High noise → flashing process, vibration, loose wiring/shielding, unstable air supply (for DP seals).
  • DP wrong sign → H/L swapped, incorrect impulse routing.

Calibration Tip (Field Practical)

Before calibrating, confirm: manifold valve positions, impulse line condition, and process stability. Calibrating a transmitter with installation faults only hides the real problem.
