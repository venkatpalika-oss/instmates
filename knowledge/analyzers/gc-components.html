Gas Chromatograph Components – Deep Dive

Technician-focused reference for field troubleshooting

1. Sampling System

  • Sample probes, filters, and conditioning
  • Pressure regulation critical for repeatability
  • Leaks cause retention time and area errors
  • Condensation causes negative or distorted peaks

2. Carrier Gas System

  • Gas purity directly affects baseline stability
  • Moisture damages columns and detectors
  • Pressure fluctuation shifts retention time

3. Columns

  • Packed vs capillary columns
  • Column aging reduces resolution
  • Temperature overshoot damages stationary phase

4. Column Oven

  • Controls separation speed
  • Uneven heating causes peak distortion
  • Oven trips common after power recovery

5. Detectors

  • FID – sensitive to hydrocarbons
  • TCD – permanent gases
  • ECD – trace-level compounds
  • Noise often caused by grounding or power issues

6. Electronics & Control

  • Signal processing and peak integration
  • Firmware affects calibration behavior
  • Power disturbances lead to false alarms

Field Reality

In most plant incidents, GC failures originate from sampling systems and utilities — not the analyzer core.
