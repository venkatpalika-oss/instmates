FTIR Analyzer – Principles & Maintenance

Category: Analyzer · FTIR · Gas Analysis

What Is an FTIR Analyzer?

FTIR (Fourier Transform Infrared) analyzers are used to measure multiple gas components simultaneously by analyzing how infrared light is absorbed by gas molecules.

FTIR analyzers are widely used in:

  • CEMS and emission monitoring systems
  • Process gas analysis
  • Environmental compliance applications
  • Multi-component gas measurement

FTIR Working Principle (Technician View)

Every gas absorbs infrared (IR) radiation at specific wavelengths. FTIR analyzers identify and quantify gases by measuring this absorption pattern.

  1. An IR source generates broadband infrared light
  2. Light passes through an interferometer
  3. Modulated IR beam passes through the sample gas cell
  4. Gas absorbs specific wavelengths
  5. Detector measures the resulting signal
  6. Software applies Fourier Transform to generate a spectrum
  7. Gas concentrations are calculated from absorption peaks

Unlike single-gas analyzers, FTIR can measure multiple gases at the same time using one optical path.

Main FTIR Analyzer Components

1. Infrared Source

  • Provides stable broadband IR radiation
  • Source aging reduces signal strength
  • Failure causes low signal or analyzer fault

2. Interferometer

  • Splits and recombines IR beam
  • Highly sensitive to vibration and alignment
  • Contamination affects spectrum quality

3. Sample Gas Cell

  • Defines optical path length
  • Longer path = higher sensitivity
  • Condensation inside cell causes false readings

4. Detector

  • Converts IR signal into electrical signal
  • Temperature stability is critical
  • Detector drift affects baseline accuracy

5. Electronics & Software

  • Performs Fourier Transform calculations
  • Applies calibration models
  • Stores spectra and concentration data

Routine Maintenance Practices

Preventive maintenance is critical for FTIR analyzers due to their optical sensitivity.

  • Inspect and clean sample filters regularly
  • Ensure sample gas temperature is above dew point
  • Verify purge air or nitrogen supply
  • Check optical signal strength trends
  • Confirm enclosure temperature stability

Common FTIR Problems in the Field

  • Moisture interference causing cross-sensitivity
  • Low signal due to dirty optics
  • Baseline drift from temperature fluctuation
  • False readings after power interruption
  • Calibration mismatch after component replacement

Basic FTIR Troubleshooting Approach

  1. Verify sample gas temperature and pressure
  2. Check for condensation in sample lines or cell
  3. Review signal intensity and spectrum quality
  4. Confirm purge gas flow and quality
  5. Re-validate calibration if optical components were disturbed

Many FTIR issues are caused by sampling and environmental conditions rather than analyzer hardware failure.

Good Practices for Long-Term Reliability

  • Maintain stable ambient temperature
  • Avoid vibration near the analyzer
  • Never open optical sections unnecessarily
  • Document all maintenance and calibration actions
