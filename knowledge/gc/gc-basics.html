Gas Chromatography (GC) – Basics

Gas Chromatography (GC) is a core analytical technique used in refineries, petrochemical plants, gas processing units, and power plants to continuously measure gas composition. This section explains GC fundamentals with technician-focused logic and real plant behavior.

GC Working Principle – Basic Flow

A GC analyzer separates gas components inside a column based on their physical and chemical properties. Each component reaches the detector at a different time, forming a chromatogram.

Important Note for Technicians

A GC analyzer is only as good as its sampling system and valve timing. Before suspecting columns, detectors, or electronics, always verify sampling integrity, pressure stability, and valve operation.
