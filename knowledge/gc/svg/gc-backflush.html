Gas Chromatograph Backflush – Working Principle

Backflush is a GC technique used to remove heavy or high-boiling components from the pre-column after cut time. This prevents contamination of the analytical column and detector, improves cycle time, and increases analyzer reliability.

Backflush Flow Sequence (Animated)

Backflush Timing Sequence

Backflush occurs only after the cut time. Light components reach the detector first; heavy components are then removed by reversing the flow.

Backflush Valve Configuration

Backflush is achieved by switching the dedicated backflush valve, reversing flow through the pre-column while isolating the analytical column.
