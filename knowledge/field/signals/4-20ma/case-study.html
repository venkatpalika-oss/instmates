Field Case Study – Stuck at 4 mA

This real incident highlights why understanding the 4–20 mA live zero concept is critical in field troubleshooting.

Plant Situation

  • Pressure transmitter on pump discharge
  • DCS reading fixed at 4.0 mA
  • Operator assumed zero pressure

Initial Wrong Assumption

The transmitter was suspected to be faulty and planned for replacement without field verification.

Troubleshooting Performed

  • Loop current measured in field: 3.98 mA
  • Process pressure confirmed present
  • Impulse line found completely plugged

Root Cause

The transmitter was healthy. The plugged impulse line caused the sensor to see zero pressure, resulting in a valid 4 mA output.

Corrective Action

  • Impulse line flushed and cleaned
  • Pressure restored
  • Loop verified normal operation

Lesson Learned

A stable 4 mA reading usually indicates a valid measurement at LRV, not a transmitter failure.
