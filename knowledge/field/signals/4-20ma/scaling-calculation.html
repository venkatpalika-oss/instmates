4–20 mA Scaling & Calculation

Scaling converts the 4–20 mA signal into meaningful engineering units such as bar, °C, %, or ppm. Every PLC, DCS, and analyzer system relies on this calculation.

Basic Concept

  • 4 mA = Lower Range Value (LRV)
  • 20 mA = Upper Range Value (URV)
  • Span = URV − LRV

Scaling Formula

Process Value =
(Current − 4) ÷ 16 × Span + LRV

This formula is universal and works for pressure, temperature, flow, level, and analyzer signals.

Worked Example

Pressure transmitter range: 0–100 bar

  • LRV = 0 bar
  • URV = 100 bar
  • Span = 100 bar

If measured current = 12 mA: 

(12 − 4) ÷ 16 × 100 = 50 bar

Common Field Errors

  • Wrong LRV / URV entered in PLC
  • Engineering units mismatch
  • 4–20 mA reversed (20–4)
  • Scaling done twice (transmitter + PLC)

Field Rule

Always confirm transmitter range before adjusting PLC or DCS scaling. Most “wrong readings” are configuration issues, not hardware faults.
