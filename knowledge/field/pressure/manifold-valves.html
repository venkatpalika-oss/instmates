Manifold Valves – Isolation & Equalization Logic

Manifold valves allow safe isolation, equalization, and calibration of pressure transmitters. Incorrect valve operation is a common cause of false readings and transmitter damage.

Types of Manifold Valves

3-Valve Manifold

Commonly used with differential pressure transmitters.

  • High-pressure isolation valve
  • Low-pressure isolation valve
  • Equalizing valve

5-Valve Manifold

Used where frequent calibration and venting are required.

  • High & low isolation valves
  • Equalizing valve
  • High & low vent / drain valves

Correct Operating Logic

Operation Isolation Valves Equalizing Valve
Normal Operation Open Closed
Calibration Closed Open
Transmitter Removal Closed Open

Common Field Mistakes

  • Opening equalizing valve during normal operation
  • Closing one isolation valve only
  • Fast valve operation causing sensor shock
  • Leaving vent valves slightly open

Field Tips from Experience

  • Operate valves slowly and in sequence
  • Tag valves clearly (H, L, EQ)
  • Verify valve position after maintenance
  • Use equalization before zero calibration
