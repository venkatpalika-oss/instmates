Pressure Measurement – Field Instrumentation

Pressure measurement is one of the most fundamental field instrumentation skills. It forms the basis for level and flow measurement and is involved in most day-to-day plant troubleshooting activities.

Types of Pressure

Understanding pressure reference is critical. Many field errors come from confusing gauge, absolute, and differential pressure.

Pressure Transmitters (PT / DP)

Pressure transmitters convert mechanical pressure into an electrical signal. Installation and impulse piping have more influence than the transmitter itself.

Impulse Lines – Real Failure Points

Most pressure measurement problems originate in impulse lines, not transmitters.

  • Wrong slope causes liquid accumulation
  • Condensation creates false DP
  • Plugging causes slow or frozen response

Manifold Valves

Incorrect manifold operation is a common cause of zero shift and calibration errors.
