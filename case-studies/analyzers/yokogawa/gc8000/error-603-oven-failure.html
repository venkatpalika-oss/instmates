Case Study: GC8000 Error 603

Oven temperature control failure during analyzer startup.

Case Snapshot

  • Analyzer: Yokogawa GC8000
  • Error Code: 603
  • Location: Refinery – Process Unit GC Shelter
  • Status: Analyzer not coming online

Observed Symptoms

  • GC stuck in startup sequence
  • Error 603 displayed after warm-up attempt
  • Oven temperature remained at ambient
  • No rise in temperature even after restart

Initial Actions Taken

  • Analyzer restarted multiple times
  • Method reloaded from backup
  • Controller reset performed

These actions did not clear the error because the issue was hardware-related.

Detailed Diagnosis

  1. Oven temperature value monitored – no increase observed
  2. Heater supply voltage checked – present
  3. RTD resistance checked – normal (~110 Ω at ambient)
  4. Heater output current found to be zero

Root Cause Identified

The oven heater relay on the oven control board was not switching, preventing heater power from reaching the oven element.

Corrective Action

  • Oven control board removed and inspected
  • Heater relay found faulty
  • Oven control board replaced
  • Analyzer restarted and oven temperature stabilized

Result After Fix

  • Error 603 cleared automatically
  • Oven reached setpoint within normal time
  • GC returned to normal analysis

Failure Logic (Simplified)

Lessons Learned

  • Error 603 is usually hardware, not configuration
  • RTD may be healthy even if oven does not heat
  • Always verify heater current, not just voltage
  • Repeated restarts do not solve heater circuit faults
