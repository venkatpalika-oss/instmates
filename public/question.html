GC baseline drifting after column change

Asked by Venkatesh · Analyzer · GC · 1 day ago

After replacing the GC column, a continuous baseline drift is observed. Oven temperature is stable and detector gases are within limits. Calibration completes successfully, but the baseline keeps drifting.

What parameters or checks should be done next?

Gas Chromatograph Baseline Drift Column Troubleshooting

Answers

After column replacement, baseline drift is commonly due to column conditioning. Check whether the column has been baked at the recommended temperature for sufficient time.

Also verify:

  • Column installation direction
  • Carrier gas purity and leaks
  • Detector reference flow stability

Answered by Rahul · GC Specialist · 12 years experience

Baseline drift can also occur if the column was exposed to moisture or oxygen during installation.

I recommend purging the column with carrier gas before heating and checking EPC pressure stability during analysis.
