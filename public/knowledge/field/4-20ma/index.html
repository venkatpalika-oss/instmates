4–20 mA Loop Fundamentals

4–20 mA signal basics written for field technicians — focusing on real wiring behavior, grounding, noise, and loop troubleshooting.

Why 4–20 mA Is Used

  • Noise immunity over long distances
  • Live zero detects cable faults
  • Simple and robust loop diagnostics

Types of 4–20 mA Loops

  • 2-wire (loop-powered)
  • 3-wire transmitters
  • 4-wire transmitters

Grounding & Noise Problems

  • Multiple grounding points cause loop instability
  • Poor shielding causes signal fluctuation
  • High resistance joints distort readings

Common Field Symptoms

  • Stable current at field, fluctuating at DCS
  • Signal jumps during motor starts
  • Zero drift after maintenance
