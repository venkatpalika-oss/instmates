Pressure Measurement – Engineering Fundamentals

Pressure measurement is one of the most fundamental field instrumentation skills. It forms the base for flow and level measurement and plays a major role in daily plant troubleshooting.

Types of Pressure

Many field errors come from misunderstanding pressure reference.

Learn more: Pressure Basics

Pressure Transmitters (PT / DP)

  • Convert pressure into 4–20 mA signal
  • Require proper impulse piping
  • Installation errors cause most failures
  • Scaling must match process design

The transmitter is rarely the root cause — installation usually is.

Deep dive: Pressure Transmitters – Internals & Field Logic

Common Pressure Field Problems

  • Pressure drifting after shutdown
  • Reading unstable during temperature change
  • Mismatch between local gauge and DCS
  • Impulse line blockage
  • Manifold valve leakage

Root cause reference: Pressure Failures – RCA Guide

Pressure Troubleshooting Logic

  1. Verify process isolation
  2. Check impulse line slope and blockage
  3. Confirm manifold valve position
  4. Compare local gauge vs transmitter
  5. Review DCS scaling
