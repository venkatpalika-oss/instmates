4–20 mA Troubleshooting Guide

Most 4–20 mA loop problems can be diagnosed using a multimeter, logical checks, and an understanding of current behavior.

Decision Tree (Start With Current)

Case-Based Troubleshooting

Case 1: 0 mA

  • Open circuit or broken cable
  • No loop power
  • Incorrect terminal wiring
  • Failed input card

Action: Check supply voltage and continuity before touching the transmitter.

Case 2: 4–20 mA but Wrong Reading

  • Incorrect range or scaling
  • Process condition misunderstood
  • LRV / URV mismatch

Action: Verify engineering units and scaling in the control system.

Case 3: > 21 mA (NAMUR Fault)

  • Internal electronics fault
  • Sensor failure
  • Configuration error

Action: Refer to diagnostics page and transmitter manual.

Case 4: Unstable / Fluctuating Signal

  • Poor grounding
  • Shield not earthed properly
  • Loose terminals
  • Electrical interference

Action: Fix wiring and grounding first — never “calibrate out” noise.

Final Verification

  1. Simulate 4–20 mA signal
  2. Confirm PLC / DCS scaling
  3. Verify alarms and displays
  4. Document findings

Field Rule

Never troubleshoot from the control room alone. Always measure the loop current.
