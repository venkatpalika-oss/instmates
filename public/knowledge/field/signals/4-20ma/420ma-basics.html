4–20 mA Basics

The 4–20 mA current loop is the most widely used analog signal in industrial instrumentation. It is simple, reliable, noise-resistant, and ideal for harsh plant environments.

What is a 4–20 mA Signal?

A 4–20 mA signal represents a process variable by varying the current flowing in a closed loop.

  • 4 mA → Minimum process value
  • 20 mA → Maximum process value

The same current flows through the transmitter, cables, and receiving device.

Why Current Instead of Voltage?

  • Cable resistance does not affect current
  • Better noise immunity
  • Long-distance transmission possible
  • Easy fault detection

Why 4 mA and Not 0 mA?

4 mA is called live zero.

  • 4 mA → Valid minimum reading
  • 0 mA → Cable break or transmitter failure

This allows technicians to distinguish between a real zero value and a fault.

Where 4–20 mA Is Used

  • Pressure transmitters
  • Temperature transmitters
  • Flow and level instruments
  • Gas and liquid analyzers

Field Rule

Always measure loop current with a multimeter before adjusting software, scaling, or replacing instruments.
