Live Zero Concept

The most important design decision in analog signaling is the use of 4 mA as live zero. This allows fault detection without additional wiring or diagnostics.

Why 4 mA Instead of 0 mA?

  • 4 mA = valid minimum measurement
  • 0 mA = cable break or dead transmitter
  • Faults are immediately detectable

With 0–20 mA systems, zero value and fault look identical.

Field Rule

Any reading below 3.6 mA or above 21 mA must be treated as a fault, not a process condition.
