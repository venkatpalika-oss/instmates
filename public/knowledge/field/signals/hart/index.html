HART Communication Protocol

Category: Field Instrumentation · Smart Transmitters · Digital Overlay

What Is HART?

HART (Highway Addressable Remote Transducer) is a hybrid communication protocol that combines traditional 4–20 mA analog signaling with digital data communication.

It allows technicians to access configuration, diagnostics, and device information without interrupting the analog signal.

  • Analog signal = process variable
  • Digital signal = configuration & diagnostics
  • Works over existing 4–20 mA wiring

How HART Works

HART uses Frequency Shift Keying (FSK) communication superimposed on the 4–20 mA signal.

  • 1200 Hz = digital “1”
  • 2200 Hz = digital “0”
  • Analog current unaffected

This allows simultaneous analog measurement and digital communication.

Why HART Is Important

  • Remote configuration without removing transmitter
  • Access to device diagnostics
  • Loop test and trim functions
  • Asset management integration

HART vs Traditional 4–20 mA

Feature 4–20 mA HART
Analog Signal ✔ Yes ✔ Yes
Digital Data ✖ No ✔ Yes
Device Diagnostics Limited Advanced
Configuration from Field No Yes
