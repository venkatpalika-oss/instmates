FTIR Analyzer – Principles, Components & Field Maintenance

Category: Analyzer · FTIR · Multi-Gas Infrared Spectroscopy

What Is an FTIR Analyzer?

FTIR (Fourier Transform Infrared) analyzers measure multiple gas components simultaneously by analyzing how gas molecules absorb infrared radiation across a wide wavelength spectrum.

Unlike single-wavelength IR analyzers, FTIR systems capture a full absorption spectrum and mathematically extract gas concentrations using Fourier Transform algorithms. This makes FTIR ideal for multi-component and complex gas mixtures.

  • CEMS (Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems)
  • Stack monitoring & environmental compliance
  • Process gas analysis with multiple components
  • Laboratory-grade spectral diagnostics in field installations

FTIR – Technician Perspective

From a field standpoint, FTIR reliability depends more on:

  • Sampling system integrity
  • Moisture and condensation control
  • Optical cleanliness
  • Temperature stability
  • Vibration isolation

In real plants, most FTIR issues originate from sampling conditions, not from optical hardware failure.

FTIR Working Principle (Step-by-Step)

Each gas molecule absorbs infrared radiation at specific frequencies. FTIR identifies gases by matching measured absorption patterns with stored reference spectra.

  1. Broadband IR source generates infrared radiation
  2. Interferometer modulates the IR beam
  3. Modulated beam passes through the sample cell
  4. Gas molecules absorb specific wavelengths
  5. Detector measures resulting signal intensity
  6. Fourier Transform converts signal into frequency spectrum
  7. Software compares spectrum with calibration models

The quality of the spectrum directly determines measurement accuracy.

Main FTIR Analyzer Components

1. Infrared Source

  • Provides stable broadband IR radiation
  • Signal strength gradually decreases with aging
  • Low intensity alarms often indicate source degradation

2. Interferometer (Heart of FTIR)

  • Splits beam into two optical paths
  • Recombines beams to create interference pattern
  • Extremely sensitive to vibration and alignment
  • Mechanical shock may cause spectral distortion

3. Sample Gas Cell

  • Defines optical path length
  • Longer path = higher sensitivity
  • Condensation causes false absorption peaks
  • Corrosive gases may damage reflective coatings

4. Detector

  • Converts IR radiation into electrical signal
  • Often temperature-controlled (Peltier cooled)
  • Drift causes baseline instability

5. Signal Processing & Software

  • Performs Fourier Transform calculations
  • Applies cross-interference correction
  • Uses chemometric models for concentration output
  • Stores diagnostic spectral data

Sampling System – The Real Critical Area

FTIR analyzers are extremely sensitive to moisture and temperature changes. Poor sampling design leads to majority of field issues.

  • Sample temperature must stay above dew point
  • Heated lines must be uniform and continuous
  • Particulate filters must be maintained regularly
  • Leakage introduces dilution errors
  • Water vapor cross-interference must be compensated

Routine Preventive Maintenance

  • Inspect and replace sample filters
  • Verify heated line temperature controllers
  • Check purge air or nitrogen quality
  • Trend optical signal intensity
  • Inspect enclosure cooling or HVAC systems
  • Review spectrum quality periodically

Common FTIR Field Problems

  • Moisture cross-sensitivity causing false gas readings
  • Baseline drift due to enclosure temperature fluctuation
  • Low signal alarm from dirty optics or aging source
  • Spurious peaks after power interruption
  • Calibration drift after replacing optical components
  • Vibration-induced spectral noise

Structured Troubleshooting Approach

  1. Confirm sample gas temperature & pressure
  2. Check heated line continuity
  3. Inspect sample cell for condensation
  4. Review signal intensity trends
  5. Compare current spectrum with reference
  6. Verify purge gas supply
  7. Revalidate calibration only if optics were disturbed

Avoid immediate recalibration without checking sampling conditions first.

Best Practices for Long-Term Reliability

  • Install vibration isolation mounts
  • Maintain stable ambient temperature
  • Ensure dry purge gas at all times
  • Never open optical section unnecessarily
  • Document spectrum changes during maintenance

FTIR analyzers are powerful but highly sensitive instruments. Clean optics + stable temperature + dry sampling = stable analyzer.
