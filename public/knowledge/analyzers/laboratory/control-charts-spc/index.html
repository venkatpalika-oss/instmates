Control Charts & Statistical Process Control (SPC)

Statistical Process Control (SPC) ensures laboratory measurement systems remain stable, repeatable, and compliant with ISO 17025 requirements.

What is a Control Chart?

  • Graphical monitoring tool
  • Tracks measurement over time
  • Detects drift and instability
  • Separates random vs systematic error

Main Types of Control Charts

X-bar Chart

  • Monitors process mean
  • Used for replicate samples
  • Detects drift over time

R Chart

  • Monitors variability
  • Shows spread of results
  • Detects instability in repeatability

Control Limit Calculation

  • Mean (x̄)
  • Standard deviation (σ)
  • Upper Control Limit (UCL = x̄ + 3σ)
  • Lower Control Limit (LCL = x̄ − 3σ)

3-sigma limits capture 99.7% of normal distribution.

Westgard Rules (Quality Control Rules)

  • 1-2s warning rule
  • 1-3s rejection rule
  • 2-2s rule
  • R-4s rule
  • 4-1s rule
  • 10x rule (systematic bias)

Violation of rules indicates need for corrective action.

Common SPC Signals

  • Trend (7 points increasing/decreasing)
  • Sudden shift
  • Point outside control limits
  • Increased variability
