ASTM D56 – Tag Closed Cup Method

ASTM D56 is used to determine the flash point of low-viscosity and volatile liquids with flash points below 93°C. It is commonly used for solvents, light fuels, and volatile petroleum fractions.

Method Principle

  • Sample placed in sealed Tag test cup
  • Controlled heating at defined rate
  • Ignition source applied at temperature intervals
  • Flash detected visually

Because of the small cup volume, heat transfer and ramp control are critical.

ASTM D56 vs ASTM D93

ASTM D56

  • Lower flash range liquids
  • Small cup design
  • Used for volatile solvents
  • Less suitable for viscous oils

ASTM D93

  • Higher flash range
  • Larger closed cup system
  • Used for diesel, lube oils
  • More robust stirring system

Critical Control Parameters

  • Heating rate compliance
  • Ignition timing accuracy
  • Proper lid sealing
  • Temperature sensor accuracy
  • Sample volume precision

A deviation in heating rate by even 0.5°C/min can alter flash point result.

Common ASTM D56 Errors

  • Early ignition due to contamination
  • Delayed flash from weak ignition source
  • Temperature overshoot
  • Improper cup cleaning
  • Incorrect ramp programming
