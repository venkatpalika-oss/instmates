FTIR Sampling System – Deep Dive

Category: Analyzer · FTIR · Sampling System

Why the FTIR Sampling System Is Critical

FTIR analyzers are extremely sensitive to moisture, temperature, pressure, and contamination. Even a perfectly calibrated FTIR analyzer will give unreliable results if the sampling system is poorly designed or maintained.

In real plant installations, most FTIR issues originate from:

  • Condensation in sample lines or gas cell
  • Improper heating or insulation
  • Moisture interference and cross-sensitivity
  • Leakage and ambient air ingress

Typical FTIR Sampling Flow

Key FTIR Sampling System Components

1. Sample Probe

  • Must extract representative gas
  • Often heated to prevent condensation
  • Plugging causes slow response and dilution
  • Incorrect insertion depth leads to false readings

2. Heated Sample Lines

Heated lines are mandatory for most FTIR applications.

  • Temperature must stay above gas dew point
  • Cold spots cause intermittent condensation
  • Power loss results in immediate measurement errors

3. Filters

  • Protect optical gas cell from particulates
  • Partial choking affects pressure stability
  • Wet filters cause adsorption and cross-interference

4. Gas Cell

  • Optical path length determines sensitivity
  • Moisture film on windows reduces IR transmission
  • Condensation causes drifting or false high readings

5. Pressure & Flow Control

  • Stable pressure improves spectral repeatability
  • Leaks introduce ambient air
  • Incorrect flow affects residence time in cell

Common FTIR Sampling Failures in Plants

  • Condensation during night or winter operation
  • Heater controller failure
  • Moisture masking gas absorption bands
  • Leaks at fittings and probes
  • Sample dilution due to ambient ingress

Field Troubleshooting Sequence

  1. Confirm probe and line temperatures
  2. Check dew point versus heater setpoint
  3. Inspect filters for moisture or blockage
  4. Leak test the entire sampling path
  5. Review FTIR spectrum for water interference

If FTIR readings change with ambient temperature, humidity, or time of day, suspect the sampling system first.
